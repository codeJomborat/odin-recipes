Sinigang na hipon ni Mama

Sinigang na Hipon is a popular Filipino dish, known for its sour and savory flavor. It’s a shrimp soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and various vegetables. The dish is perfect for a comforting meal, especially during rainy days..

Ingredients

  • 500g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 2–3 long green chili peppers (siling pang-sigang)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 1-2 cups radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1-2 cups sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2–3 cups water
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or to taste)
  • 1 tbsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1 pack of sinigang mix (optional, or fresh tamarind if available)
  • 1 tbsp oil for sautéing

Steps to Cook Sinigang na Hipon:

  1. In a pot, sauté onions and tomatoes in oil until softened.
  2. Add water and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, add the sinigang mix or fresh tamarind (if using).
  3. Season with fish sauce and salt. Let it simmer for 5–10 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  4. Once the broth is flavorful, add the radish, string beans, and green chili peppers. Cook for another 5–7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Lastly, add the shrimp and cook for about 3–5 minutes, or until they turn pink and fully cooked.
  6. Add the kangkong (water spinach) and simmer for an additional 2 minutes.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!
